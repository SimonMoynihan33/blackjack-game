BlackJack

Are you ready to play?

Rules:

Objective:

The goal is to beat the dealer by having a hand value closer to 21 without exceeding it.

Card Values:

Number cards: Face value (e.g., a 7 of Hearts is worth 7 points).

Face cards (King, Queen, Jack): Each worth 10 points.

Ace: Can be worth 1 or 11 points

Game Setup:

The game uses one or more standard 52-card decks.

Players are dealt two cards face up.

The dealer is dealt two cards: one face up and one face down (the "hole card").

Gameplay:

Player Actions:

Hit: Take another card to increase your hand's value.

Stand: Keep your current hand and end your turn.

Double Down: Double your initial bet and take exactly one more card.

Split: If your first two cards have the same value, you can split them into two separate hands (requires doubling your bet).

Surrender: Forfeit half your bet and end your round (optional, if allowed).

Dealer Actions:

The dealer must draw cards (hit) until their hand totals at least 17.

The dealer must stand on a hand of 17 or more (depending on house rules, they may hit on a "soft 17" — a hand with an Ace counting as 11).

Winning Conditions

Blackjack: If your first two cards total exactly 21 (e.g., Ace + 10 or face card), you have a "Blackjack" and win 1.5 times your bet (unless the dealer also has Blackjack, which is a tie or "push").

Closer to 21: You win if your hand value is closer to 21 than the dealer's hand, without exceeding 21.

Dealer Busts: If the dealer's hand exceeds 21, all remaining players win.

Bust: If your hand exceeds 21, you automatically lose.

Additional Rules

Push: If your hand and the dealer's hand have the same value, it's a tie, and your bet is returned.

Insurance: If the dealer’s face-up card is an Ace, you can place an "insurance" bet (half your original bet) to protect against the dealer having Blackjack. If the dealer has Blackjack, the insurance bet pays 2:1 but you lose your original bet.

Soft vs. Hard Hands:

A soft hand includes an Ace counted as 11 (e.g., Ace + 6 = soft 17).

A hard hand has no Ace or counts the Ace as 1 (e.g., 10 + 7 = hard 17).

Betting

Players place their bets before the cards are dealt.

Minimum and maximum bet limits vary depending on the table or casino.
